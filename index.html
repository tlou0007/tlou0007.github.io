What is Wizards Chess?

Wizards Chess is one of the most popular sports in the wizarding world, and for good reason.

Imagine Chess but more extreme, and you'll have Wizards Chess. Two people face off across an enchanted chess board where they command pieces that can move on their own accord. Confrontation results in explosions, smashed pieces scattering the board with each attck.

As Australia's leading school of Wizards Chess, we're here to introduce you to the basics - continue reading to learn more.

Rules & How To Play

For the most part, Wizards Chess is quite similar to it's muggle counterpart, except for the exploding and sentient pieces of course. In the rules here there is mention of "legal" moves- these will be explained in further detail in "The Pieces" setion below.

To move the pieces, you must speak their names and the square you wish for it to move to.

  • 1 One player moves at a time, taking alternating turns
  • 6 Movement of the pieces must not be inteffered with - verbal commands only
  • 2 The person with the white pieces will be the first to move
  • 7 If a King is threatened but can protect himself, it is Check
  • 3 Movement is required on every round and this movement must be legal
  • 8 The King cannot be left in Check, the threat must be eliminated
  • 4 Each piece has it's own method of movement that MUST be obeyed
  • 9 When the King is threatened and cannot be protected, this is called Checkmate
  • 5 A player may listen to the advice provided by the chess pieces, but it is avised that this may not always be a reliable source
  • 10 If it is a players turn and they are in check but there are not legal moves, this is Stalemate and the game ends in a draw
The Pieces

(They're just like the muggle version!)

  • Pawn

    Can move forward 1 square if unoccupied, except on the inital play where they can move 2.
    Cannot move backwards.
    Can capture an enemy on either square diagnolly infront.

    1 Point
  • Knight

    Can only move in an "L" or "7" shape, laid out at any vertical or horizontal angle.
    Can move one square along any rank or file and then at an angle.

    3 Points
  • Bishop

    Can move any number of vacant squares in any diagnol direction.
    Cannot move in straight lines.



    3 Points
  • Rook

    Can move any number of vacant squares either horizontally or vertically.
    Can also be moved when castling.


    5 Points
  • Queen

    Can move any number of vacant squares diagnally, horizontally or vertically.




    9 Points
  • King

    Can move one square diagnally, horizontally or vertically.
    Can make one special move per game, called castling.


    End

Who Are We?

We are Terrestial's School Of Wizards Chess, best know among wizards and witches just as Terrestial. We are a dedicated team of trained sportwizards and sportswitches, as well as dedicated staff members who work to provide the best experience to our teams as possible.

Here at Terrestial we believe that while Wizards Chess is a well respected sport, it should also be available to those of all ages and abilities.
(However, due to the nature of the sport however we do suggest you are over the age of 13.)

We've made it our job to create an easily accesible resource full of information to those who are just starting out, or our trained professionals who want to brush op on their knowledge or a few rusty skills.


We have compiled a compendium of resources and a wealth of knowledge so you can further explore this incredible sport. We offer a variety of events that you can participate in as either as a player or a spectator, as well as communtiy fundraisers and general peer activities.

Learn About Our Teams

Meet our teams, located across Australia

Ember

Ember was founded in 1970 by Charleston James, just north of Melbourne. Ember was the first all girls team in Australia to win a state tournament!

Aether

Aether was founded in 1997 by Helena Darbinian, in Perth. Aether is our oldest team, holding a total of 23 state championship titles!

Glade

Glade was founded in 2001 by Marja Atsma, in Adelaide. Glade is our hoemgroup winner, known for always winning our home team tournaments (we call it favouritism!)

Current

Current was founded just recently in 2020 by Fred Morris. Current was a result of covid, actually founded online as a way to bring the community together in troubling times!
